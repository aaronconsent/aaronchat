Who's behind the grades

Meet Aaron.

I've spent 20-plus years in marketing — most of it in the web-hosting and SaaS world, where the audience was the whole planet and the competition never slept. I was Chief Business Officer at cPanel, the software that runs a huge slice of the world's websites, and I've been a founder or C-level exec at a string of tech companies since.

Today I'm co-founder & CMO of Consent Resolve — a privacy-first way for contractors to turn anonymous website visitors into real, consented leads — and I run this agency for home-service pros around Lake Livingston.

So why does someone who marketed software to the entire world do marketing for East Texas plumbers and HVAC shops? Because the same playbook that grows a software company works for a local shop: measure what actually matters, fix what's broken, and report it in plain English — calls and booked jobs, not impressions and vibes. Most local shops have never had someone with that background in their corner. Now they do.

Why the report card

It started with a question nobody could answer.

Ask any contractor around the lake "how do you stack up against the other guys on Google?" and you'd get a shrug. Nobody knew — not really. So I built the thing that answers it: the Lake Livingston Service Pro Report, a public site that grades every service business in Polk, Walker, San Jacinto and Trinity counties, every week, on the exact signals homeowners use to pick who gets the call.

The agency exists because of what the data showed: in almost every trade, the #1 spot is held with numbers a serious local shop can catch inside a year. Somebody's going to do the catching. I work for those shops.

The rules I work by

Three promises.

1

Same rubric for everyone

Clients get graded by the exact same public formula as everyone else. If our clients' grades don't climb, everyone can see it — including you, before you hire us.

2

One shop per trade per town

I won't work for two plumbers fighting over the same customers. First one in gets the seat; everyone else gets the free report card.

3

Month to month, always

No annual contracts. The public scoreboard is the retention strategy — if the grade stops climbing, you should leave.
